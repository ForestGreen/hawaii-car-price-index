Overview: Should You Ship or Buy?

One of the biggest decisions when relocating to Hawaii is whether to ship your mainland vehicle or purchase one locally. This decision has significant financial implications—the difference can exceed $5,000 to $10,000 depending on your specific situation.

The answer isn't universal. Your best choice depends on:

  • Vehicle Value: What you'd get for the vehicle on the mainland
  • Hawaii Market Pricing: What equivalent vehicles cost in Hawaii
  • Shipping Costs: Port-specific costs and method selection
  • Your Timeline: How quickly you need a vehicle
  • Vehicle Condition: Age, mileage, maintenance history
  • Emotional Factors: Attachment to a specific vehicle
Quick Rule of Thumb: If your mainland vehicle is worth less than 70-75% of Hawaii equivalent pricing after accounting for shipping costs, buying in Hawaii usually makes more financial sense.

Detailed Cost Breakdown: Shipping vs. Buying

Shipping Your Mainland Vehicle to Hawaii

When shipping a vehicle, you'll encounter several cost categories beyond just the ocean transport.

Cost Category Typical Range Notes & Variables
Ocean Transport $900 - $1,800 Open transport $900-$1,300; Enclosed $1,500-$2,500. Varies by origin port and destination island.
Port Handling & Fees $300 - $500 Includes unloading, documentation, and terminal handling. Honolulu typically cheaper than neighbor islands.
Customs & Import Paperwork $150 - $300 Documentation fees for federal and state import requirements. Some brokers include this.
Emission Testing & Modifications $300 - $800 Hawaii's strict standards may require modifications. Pre-import inspection essential.
Registration & Title Transfer $300 - $500 Hawaii DMV registration, title transfer, and vehicle inspection fees.
Transport from Port $100 - $300 Getting vehicle from port to your location. Typically negotiated with shipper.
Insurance (in transit) $200 - $400 Transit insurance during shipping. Some shippers include basic coverage.
TOTAL SHIPPING COSTS $2,250 - $4,700 Typical total for mainland to Hawaii shipping and import.

Buying Locally in Hawaii

When purchasing a vehicle in Hawaii, costs include the vehicle itself plus various fees and taxes.

Cost Category Typical Range Notes & Variables
Vehicle Purchase Price Varies Hawaii vehicles typically 15-20% more than mainland for equivalent models.
Pre-Purchase Inspection $150 - $250 Professional inspection by trusted mechanic. Essential in Hawaii due to salt air.
Registration & Title $300 - $500 Hawaii DMV registration based on vehicle value and weight.
General Excise Tax (GET) 4.712% Hawaii applies GET to used vehicle sales. Some dealers include, some don't.
Extended Warranty (Optional) $500 - $2,000 Some dealers offer extended warranties. Negotiate or purchase independently.
Initial Maintenance $200 - $400 First oil change, fluid checks, and preliminary maintenance.
TOTAL ADDITIONAL COSTS $1,250 - $3,650 Not including vehicle purchase price; GET included if applicable.

Pros & Cons: Shipping vs. Buying

Shipping Your Vehicle

Advantages

  • Keep your familiar, trusted vehicle
  • No unknown vehicle history concerns
  • Maintain all your vehicle customizations
  • Avoid aggressive dealer negotiations
  • Better value if vehicle is newer/low mileage
  • No salt-air damage to inspect concerns

Disadvantages

  • Higher total cost in most scenarios
  • 4-8 week wait time for delivery
  • Potential damage during shipping
  • Emission modifications may be required
  • Need to arrange mainland sale or storage
  • Transit insurance necessary
  • Shipping fees non-refundable if damage occurs

Buying Locally in Hawaii

Advantages

  • Immediate vehicle availability
  • Can inspect vehicle in person
  • No shipping costs or delays
  • Often lower total cost
  • Easier financing options
  • Local mechanic relationships for service
  • Can test drive before commitment
  • Better for urgent needs

Disadvantages

  • 15-20% higher prices than mainland
  • Limited inventory (especially unique models)
  • Higher salt-air corrosion risk on older vehicles
  • Must navigate aggressive dealer tactics
  • Less selection across islands
  • May need to travel between islands to compare
  • Title transfers take time

Decision Matrix: When to Ship vs. Buy

Vehicle over 10 years old, over 120k miles Buy Local Older vehicles not worth shipping. Hawaii market often has better deals on used inventory.

Real-World Examples: Shipping vs. Buying

Example 1: New Vehicle Relocation (Favorable for Shipping)

Scenario: Software engineer relocating to Hawaii with 2023 Honda CR-V, 35,000 miles, currently worth $28,000 on mainland market.

  • Mainland Value: $28,000
  • Shipping Costs: $1,100 (open transport) + $1,250 (fees/handling/registration) = $2,350
  • Total Shipping Cost: $30,350
  • Hawaii Equivalent CR-V: $33,200 (17.5% premium)
  • Hawaii Total Cost: $33,550
  • Winner: Ship — Saves $3,200

Example 2: Used Vehicle Relocation (Favorable for Buying)

Scenario: Family relocating with 2012 Ford F-150, 145,000 miles, currently worth $16,000 on mainland.

  • Mainland Value: $16,000
  • Shipping Costs: $1,100 + $1,250 = $2,350
  • Total Shipping Cost: $18,350
  • Hawaii Equivalent F-150: $19,200 (17.5% premium)
  • Hawaii Total Cost: $19,550
  • Winner: Ship slightly — Saves $1,200
  • Practical Consideration: Given truck age/mileage, buying local allows inspection for salt damage. Very close call; inspect vehicles carefully.

Example 3: Old Vehicle Relocation (Favorable for Buying)

Scenario: Teacher relocating with 2008 Toyota Camry, 185,000 miles, worth $9,500 on mainland.

  • Mainland Value: $9,500
  • Shipping Costs: $1,100 + $1,250 = $2,350
  • Total Shipping Cost: $11,850
  • Hawaii Equivalent Camry (similar age/mileage): $11,100 (17.5% premium)
  • Hawaii Total Cost: $11,350
  • Winner: Buy Local — Saves $500
  • Practical Consideration: High-mileage vehicles are risky to ship. Better inspection opportunity buying local. Financial advantage too small to justify shipping risk.

Example 4: Military PCS with High-Value Vehicle

Scenario: Military officer relocating with 2022 Toyota 4Runner, 28,000 miles, worth $42,000.

  • Mainland Value: $42,000
  • Shipping Costs: $1,100 + $1,500 = $2,600
  • Total Shipping Cost: $44,600
  • Hawaii Equivalent 4Runner: $49,350 (17.5% premium)
  • Hawaii Total Cost: $49,750
  • Winner: Ship — Saves $5,150
  • Military Consideration: DITY (Do-It-Yourself) moves may provide transportation allowance covering shipping costs. Check with military for reimbursement options.

Complete Shipping Guide: Step-by-Step

1. Prepare Your Vehicle for Shipping

  • Get a pre-shipping inspection to document condition
  • Take clear photos/video of vehicle from all angles
  • Drain fuel to 1/4 tank or less
  • Check tire pressure and condition
  • Top off other fluids (oil, coolant, brake fluid)
  • Remove personal items from vehicle
  • Disable alarms and ensure electrical systems work

2. Research and Choose a Shipping Company

  • Get quotes from multiple auto transport companies
  • Verify insurance coverage (at least $100,000)
  • Check company reviews on BBB and independent sites
  • Compare open vs. enclosed transport options
  • Ask about origin and destination ports
  • Confirm timeline and delivery window
  • Ask about additional fees (destination delivery, fuel surcharge, etc.)

3. Book Your Shipment

  • Get written quotes before committing
  • Understand cancellation policies
  • Confirm final pricing in writing
  • Ask about deposit and payment terms
  • Get tracking information and expected delivery dates

4. At Time of Pickup

  • Document vehicle condition with time-stamped photos
  • Get itemized list of any existing damage
  • Verify mileage reading matches your records
  • Confirm shipper has title or power of attorney
  • Get receipt and tracking numbers

5. Monitor Shipment

  • Track shipment progress regularly
  • Confirm estimated arrival dates
  • Ensure proper handling notifications
  • Have contact information ready for any issues

6. Receive Vehicle in Hawaii

  • Get vehicle released from port/broker
  • Document condition immediately with photos
  • Check mileage and condition match expectations
  • Report any damage immediately
  • Arrange Hawaii title transfer and registration

Guide to Buying Locally in Hawaii

1. Determine Your Budget

  • Research equivalent vehicles on mainland to understand typical pricing
  • Add 15-20% to mainland prices for Hawaii premium
  • Factor in registration, inspection, and insurance costs
  • Set comfortable maximum price and stick to it

2. Research Island Options

  • Oahu has largest inventory and most competitive pricing
  • Maui and Big Island offer good selection but higher prices
  • Kauai has limited inventory but may have specific deals
  • Consider travel time to visit multiple dealers

3. Search Available Inventory

  • Check local dealer websites and online marketplaces
  • Use online price guides to understand typical Hawaii pricing
  • Check multiple sources: dealers, private sellers, auctions
  • Set up notifications for matching vehicles

4. Make First Contact with Dealers

  • Call to verify vehicle is still available
  • Ask for complete vehicle history (title, accident history, service records)
  • Inquire about financing options and terms
  • Ask about warranty options (important in Hawaii)

5. In-Person Inspection

  • Examine exterior carefully for rust and corrosion
  • Check undercarriage thoroughly—salt air is insidious
  • Inspect door seals, window rubber, and weatherstripping
  • Test all electrical systems (windows, locks, climate control)
  • Take comprehensive vehicle history report (CARFAX/AutoCheck)

6. Professional Mechanic Inspection

  • Always get pre-purchase inspection by independent mechanic
  • Use mechanic familiar with island-specific issues
  • Get written report documenting all findings
  • Use inspection report for price negotiation

7. Negotiate Final Price

  • Use inspection report and market research as leverage
  • Get multiple quotes from different dealers
  • Negotiate total price including all fees
  • Don't hesitate to walk away for better deals

8. Complete Documentation and Transfer

  • Verify title is clean and in seller's name
  • Complete bill of sale and title transfer
  • Arrange Hawaii registration immediately
  • Get inspection sticker if required

Key Takeaways

  • Calculate total costs for both options—don't assume shipping is cheaper
  • Newer, low-mileage vehicles favor shipping; older vehicles favor local purchase
  • Use the interactive calculator above to run your specific numbers
  • Consider timing needs—buying local is instant; shipping takes 4-8 weeks
  • Hawaii prices run 15-20% higher than mainland—budget accordingly for local purchase
  • Shipping costs more than ocean transport alone—add 2-3x for all fees
  • If buying local, Oahu offers best selection and pricing of all islands
  • Always get professional pre-purchase inspections whether shipping or buying
  • Salt air damage is real—inspect carefully for rust and corrosion
  • Military personnel may qualify for shipping allowances—check with your branch
